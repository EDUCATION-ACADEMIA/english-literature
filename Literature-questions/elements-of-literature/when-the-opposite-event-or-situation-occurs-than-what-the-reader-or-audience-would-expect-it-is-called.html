When the opposite event or situation occurs than what the reader or audience would expect, it is called

(A) allusion

(B) ** irony

(C) suspense

(D) flashback

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Situational irony occurs when there is a difference between what is expected to happen and what actually happens. For example, a fire station burning down is a case of situational irony. Verbal irony is when a character says something that is different from what he or she really means, or how he or she really feels.

Concept note-2: -Situational irony Situational irony is when the outcome of a situation is totally different from what people expect. This type of irony is a literary technique that’s riddled with contradictions and contrasts.

Concept note-3: -Situational irony examples occur when the opposite of what the reader expects, happens in the story. Verbal irony refers to a character’s words. However, situational irony occurs when the situation is in contrast to what is expected.

Concept note-4: -Irony occurs when something that is said or the expected outcome of a situation is different from what actually happens. It’s a rhetorical and literary device that comes in several formats.

Concept note-5: -Dramatic irony. Also known as tragic irony, this is when a writer lets their reader know something that a character does not. Comic irony. This is when irony is used to comedic effect-such as in satire. Situational irony. Verbal irony. 03-Sept-2021
