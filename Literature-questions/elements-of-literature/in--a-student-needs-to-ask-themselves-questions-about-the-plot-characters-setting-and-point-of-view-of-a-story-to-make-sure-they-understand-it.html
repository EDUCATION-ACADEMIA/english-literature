In ____ a student needs to ask themselves questions about the plot, characters, setting and point-of view of a story to make sure they understand it.

(A) Predicting

(B) ** Questioning

(C) Analyzing

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An effective story setting connects the characters to the plot, and ties together the story’s themes and events. Builds meaning to the narrative. Without the setting of a story, there may not be as much meaning to be gleaned since setting provides context. Elicits emotional response in the reader.

Concept note-2: -Exposition Exposition is the beginning of the story and prepares the way for upcoming events to unfold. It is in this part of the plot, where major characters are introduced, the setting is established, and major conflicts of the story are revealed.

Concept note-3: -What planet, country, region, and town is my story set in? What year is it? In what season or time of year does my story take place? What is the culture like in my story? What is the political climate like in my story? 16-Jan-2018

Concept note-4: -Plot: Plot is what happens in the story. Setting: Setting includes the time and place of the story’s events. Character: Characters are those who play a role in the story. Conflict: Conflict represents the problem in the story. Theme: Theme is the main point of the story. 12-Aug-2021
