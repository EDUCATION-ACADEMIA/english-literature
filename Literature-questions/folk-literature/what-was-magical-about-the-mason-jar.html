What was magical about the mason jar?

(A) The mason jar could “catch anything.” All Jack had to do was say, “Wickety wack, into my sack.

(B) The mason jar could “catch anything.” It caught death when Jack filled it with water and stood at the head of the bed.

(C) The mason jar could indicate if a person would live or die. It showed death standing at the foot of the bed when Jack filled it with water.

(D) ** The mason jar could indicate if a person would live or die. It showed death standing at the head of the bed when Jack filled it with water.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A Mason jar, also known as a canning jar or fruit jar, is a glass jar used in home canning to preserve food. It was named after American tinsmith John Landis Mason, who patented it in 1858. The jar’s mouth has a screw thread on its outer perimeter to accept a metal ring or “band".

Concept note-2: -John Landis Mason invented the Mason Jar on November 30, 1858. In 1884, Ball Corporation began manufacturing glass home-canning jars, the product that established Ball as a household name and licensed Mason’s design. There are few everyday objects more iconic than the Ball Mason jar.

Concept note-3: -The Mason jar was first invented by John Landis Mason. He created and patented a glass jar to be used in home canning and food preservation. That was back in 1858.

Concept note-4: -a glass jar with a wide mouth and an airtight screw top, much used in home canning. [1880–85, Amer.; named after John L. Mason, 19th-century American who patented it in 1858]
