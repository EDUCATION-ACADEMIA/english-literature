DYSTOPIAN LITERATURE

[SOURCES]
In “The Veldt", the 3rd person omniscient point of view allows the reader to ____

(A) ** see into Lydia & George Hadley’s mind and reveal their fear of the nursery & the lions.

(B) occasionally hear only Lydia Hadley’s thoughts about her children’s obsession with the veldt & the nursery

(C) see the story through the eyes of Peter and Wendy, as they narrate their discontent with their parents.

(D) only observe the action of the story and infer what the characters are thinking and feeling.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In the story, “The Veldt” author, Ray Bradbury, writes the story in third person using limited omniscient.

Concept note-2: -Lydia spends most of this story worrying about the nursery: she’s “tense” about Africa (22), she’s worried that the lions might get out (60), she worries that Peter changed the nursery somehow (89).

Concept note-3: -In “The Veldt, ” Bradbury’s purpose as an author is to show the dangers that are inherent in letting technology have a foothold in our lives. Bradbury achieves this purpose through exaggerating the tensions among the Hadley family.

Concept note-4: -Lydia is George’s wife. She is the first to perceive the negative effects of the Happylife Home, and wishes she once again had a “purpose”, which she feels the fully-automated house has stolen from her. She is also genuinely frightened by the realistic power of the nursery.
