If you have taken an information from a certain website, do not forget to cite it in your paper as your source.

(A) discuss

(B) underline

(C) appreciate

(D) ** acknowledge

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If you have taken an information from a certain website, do not forget to CITE it in your paper as your source. 5. One way of citing your sources is to include a bibliography of these sources in a separate page of your research paper.

Concept note-2: -Citing sources properly is essential to avoiding plagiarism in your writing. Not citing sources properly could imply that the ideas, information, and phrasing you are using are your own, when they actually originated with another author. Plagiarism doesn’t just mean copy and pasting another author’s words.

Concept note-3: -You should cite the sources of information you use in your academic work because: Citing makes your work more credible. Citing tells your reader where you found your information. Citing allows your reader to learn more, beginning with your sources.

Concept note-4: -You must cite the source every time you incorporate research, words, ideas, data, or information that is not your own (2). While you are synthesizing and often summarizing many pieces of information, you must cite any concept that is not your own.

Concept note-5: -In general, if it’s your words, your opinion, your photo, or your graph, of course, you don’t need to cite it.
