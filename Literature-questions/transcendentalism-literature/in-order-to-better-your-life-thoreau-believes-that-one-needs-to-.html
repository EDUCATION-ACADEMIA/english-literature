In order to better your life, Thoreau believes that one needs to ____

(A) Be frittered away with details.

(B) ** Simplify, simplify!

(C) Build your own cabin.

(D) First hit rock bottom as he did.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -“As you simplify your life, the laws of the universe will be simpler; solitude will not be solitude, poverty will not be poverty, nor weakness, ” he said. In his book Walden, he described how he would go off by himself for weeks at a time in order to clear his mind and focus on the lessons life has to teach us.

Concept note-2: -In that book, Thoreau says, “Simplify, simplify.” I think we often go through life exhausted because we take on too many things, bring too much complexity to our days, and don’t have time to enjoy life. We are in need of simplifying our lives. I learned this when I was in college, and it changed my life.

Concept note-3: -Simplicity, simplicity, simplicity! I say, let your affairs be as two or three, and not a hundred or a thousand; instead of a million count by half a dozen, and keep your accounts on your thumb nail… Simplify, Simplify.

Concept note-4: -Thoreau did not wish to be chained to the economy, so he practised self-reliance; he did not wish to be slave to artificial material desires, so he practised self-discipline; and he did not wish to live what was not life, so he avoided wasting his precious time working to acquire more than he needed.

Concept note-5: -He believed that lives lived in civilization become unnecessarily complex. Living simply meant secluding yourself form other humans and being in nature. Why does Thoreau insist people need to simplify their lives? To live simply, wisely, and independently.
