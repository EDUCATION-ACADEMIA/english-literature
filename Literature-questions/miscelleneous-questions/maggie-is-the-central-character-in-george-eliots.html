[SOURCES]
Maggie is the central character in George Eliot’s:

(A) Adam Bede

(B) Middle March

(C) ** The Mill on the Floss

(D) Silas Morner

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Maggie is a talented and intelligent girl growing up at Dorlcote Mill and she is the protagonist of this story. The novel follows Maggie when she grows up from a hotheaded and smart child into an outstanding and unconventional young lady. She grows up between strict social customs and yields them.

Concept note-2: -Nervous and maladjusted, Maggie is a figure of purity, uncorrupted by selfishness or complex emotional needs. Severely burned in a house fire when she was a child, her scarred, ugly appearance hides her sympathetic, generous nature.

Concept note-3: -Maggie Tulliver – a dark-complexioned miller’s daughter. A clever and impetuous child who feels deep passions, but forces herself to suppress them for the good of others. Tom Tulliver – Maggie’s brother.

Concept note-4: -The Importance of Sympathy The Mill on the Floss is not a religious novel, but it is highly concerned with a morality that should function among all people and should aspire to a compassionate connection with others through sympathy.

Concept note-5: -Maggie is bold and independent, but she frequently submits to her family’s wishes, even when they cause her pain. She longs to find a better life and a better future, but she stubbornly clings to her painful past. She wants love more than anything, but she gives it up after finding it.
