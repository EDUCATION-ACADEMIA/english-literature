“Vacant eye-like windows” are an example of what type of figurative language?

(A) metaphor

(B) simile

(C) ** personification

(D) hyperbole

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In ‘’The Fall of the House of Usher, ” Poe uses personification, which consists of attributing human characteristics to inanimate objects. Through this figure of speech, the narrator sets a gloomy tone: ‘’the vacant eye-like windows.

Concept note-2: -Personification: What Does It Mean? When something is personified, it means it is talked or written about as if it were a person. Put another way, personification is a literary device (a type of metaphor) in which human characteristics are figuratively applied to inanimate objects or abstractions.

Concept note-3: -The vacant eyelike windows could describe the shape of the windows, or it could mean that eyes later in the short story will be vacant. No thoughts, dreams, or memories are happening behind those eyes, no life at all is taking place in there. Again, this detail symbolizes something to come later on in the story.

Concept note-4: -A metaphor is a literary device that makes a comparison directly between two things that are not in actuality the same. Poe’s narrator compares the neighbor’s eye through the metaphor: “He had the eye of a vulture–a pale blue eye, with a film over it.

Concept note-5: -Answer and Explanation: The most prominent example of personification in “Fall of the House of Usher” appears at the tale’s beginning. Upon his arrival at the house, the narrator observes that the house’s windows look like blank, staring eyes. These “eye-like” windows personify the house as a person.
