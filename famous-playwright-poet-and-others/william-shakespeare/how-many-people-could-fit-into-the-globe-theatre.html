How many people could fit into The Globe Theatre?

(A) 1000

(B) ** 3000

(C) 200

(D) 5000

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It could hold up to 3, 000 people. By May 1599, the new theatre was ready to be opened. Burbage named it the Globe after the figure of Hercules carrying the globe on his back-for in like manner the actors carried the Globe’s framework on their backs across the Thames.

Concept note-2: -Shape is an icosagon, a 20 sided polygon. Can hold 1, 570 people, 700 standing and the rest seated. The twelve signs of the zodiac are painted on this roof over the stage (which we call ‘The Heavens’). Built from oak beams, lime-plaster walls and a water-reed thatched roof.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: The only sections in the Globe Theater with actual seats were in the three galleries and balcony areas. The gallery seating areas were broken down into lower, middle, and upper and cost approximately six pennies, with a cushion costing extra.

Concept note-4: -It stood more than 30 feet (9 metres) high, with three levels of seating in its galleries. Audience access was either through two narrow passageways under the galleries into the standing room of the yard around the stage or up two external stair towers into the rear of the galleries.

Concept note-5: -Shakespeare’s Globe Theatre Stands 400 Years and Only Yards Away From the Original. Shakespeare’s Globe Theatre Was Rebuilt to be as Similar to the Original Globe as Possible.
