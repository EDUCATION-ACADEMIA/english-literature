MACBETH

Lady Macbeth cannot kill King Duncan because he looked too much like her father while he was sleeping. How does this fact develop her character?

(A) ** It makes her a round, dynamic character because she is more complex than we originally thought

(B) It reinforces that she is a flat, static character who is totally heartless and unreedemable

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -According to Lady Macbeth, Duncan looked like her own father. For instance, in the play, she says, “Had he not resembled / My father as he slept, I had done’t” (2.2. 16-17). Thus, she didn’t want to kill Duncan because killing him would seem as if she killed her own father and that decision would give her nightmares.

Concept note-2: -He then considers the reasons why he ought not to kill Duncan: Macbeth is Duncan’s kinsman, subject, and host; moreover, the king is universally admired as a virtuous ruler.

Concept note-3: -Lady Macbeth waits in agitation for Macbeth to kill Duncan. She comments that had the sleeping Duncan not looked like her father she would have killed him herself. Macbeth enters. He’s killed Duncan and Duncan’s attendants.

Concept note-4: -After Duncan’s murder, Lady Macbeth’s role is of comforter and protector of Macbeth, rather than instigator of murder, and her character becomes more sympathetic.
