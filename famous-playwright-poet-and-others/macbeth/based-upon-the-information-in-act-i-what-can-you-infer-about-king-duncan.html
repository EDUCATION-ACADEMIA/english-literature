MACBETH

Based upon the information in Act I, what can you infer about King Duncan?

(A) He is a weak leader.

(B) He is a shrewd military planner.

(C) He is reluctant to join his men in battle.

(D) ** He placed a high value on loyalty and bravery.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Based upon the information in Act 1, what can you infer about king duncan? he places a high value on bravery and loyalty.

Concept note-2: -The service and the loyalty I owe, In doing it, pays itself. When Macbeth tells Duncan, “The service and loyalty I owe, in doing it, pays itself, ” he’s riffing on the proverb, “Virtue is its own reward.”

Concept note-3: -Duncan rewards Macbeth for his bravery for defeating the rebels by inviting him to the ceremony where he tells Malcom that he’s next in line for the throne. Duncan said this in front of Macbeth, which made him mad since the witches already told him that he was going to be next in line for the throne.

Concept note-4: -why is king duncan so grateful to Macbeth? Macbeth saved King Duncan’s kingdom from rebels and the Norwegian king.
