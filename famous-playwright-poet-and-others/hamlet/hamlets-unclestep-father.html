HAMLET

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

Hamlet’s uncle/step-father

(A) Polonius

(B) Laertes

(C) ** Claudius

(D) Reynaldo

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Claudius is the new king and Hamlet’s uncle. Hamlet does not like Claudius because he marries Gertrude, his mother, and because Hamlet’s father dies. The ghost of Hamlet’s father appears to Hamlet to tell him to seek revenge on his murderer.

Concept note-2: -It tells him that Hamlet’s father did not die naturally, but was murdered by his brother Claudius. As the king slept, Claudius put poison into his ear, causing the king a painful death. The ghost tells Hamlet to take revenge on Claudius for murdering his father.

Concept note-3: -Claudius is the antagonist (the enemy of the main character) in the play Hamlet. Claudius is a morally weak villain who values power and material things more than he values others. He differs from other men in the play because he is cunning, lacks morals, and is manipulative.

Concept note-4: -Gertrude and Claudius. Claudius is the brother of Old Hamlet and Hamlet’s uncle. After his brother’s death, he married Gertrude and became the King of Denmark and criticises Hamlet for being too upset over the death of his father.
